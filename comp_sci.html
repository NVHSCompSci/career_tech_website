Computer Science
Overview of Course

First Year: In the first year of Computer Science, you will learn the basics of web design and take AP Computer Science Principles (APCSP) in JavaScript.
Second Year: In your second year of Computer Science, you will learn Web Development which involves HTML and CSS combined and then Cybersecurity which is divided into two period blocks for each respectively.

Benefits of Computer Science

There are many benefits in taking Computer Science such as learning a variety of different coding languages, thinking critically, and you get to have a chance to explore and figure out your passion in computer science within the parameters of the class.

Prerequisites

There are no required prerequisites for enrolling into Computer Science. It is however suggested to take "Intro to Programming" class (not required) and to be comfortable with basic math functions and being able to graph on a basic x and y graph.

College Credits

Currently, there are no college credits past the AP Computer Science Principles in JavaScript.

Time Commitment

For every career tech program offered at Northview, two years of commitment is expected. For Computer Science, it is a double block class for the full academic year, for two years. Overall, the workload in the class is mostly well paced with the ability to easily catch up if falling behind.

Competitions

The main competition that is associated with this program is the BPA (Business Professionals of America) Competition with the first year of competing ranking third regionally.

Internships

While there are internships planned for the future, at the moment there are none available. In the classroom, students do hands-on projects such as making their own websites, collaborations between other classes, and building things to interact with the students' code such as Raspberry Pi projects for every student.

Job Skills

First Year: In your first year, you will mainly learn how to use HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. You will also dip into other languages like SQL and the possibility of your own choice of self-guided learning.
Second Year: In your second year, you will further your understanding of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript during your first hour. In your second hour, you will be learning about Cybersecurity and how it affects you, others, its history, hacking and anti hacking measures, and how to implement it.

Certifications and Credentials

There are several certifications and or credentials you can get during the program such as:

  • Web Development Certiport Information Technology Specialist:
    • HTML & CSS (CR28)
    • CIW Advanced HTML5 & CSS3 Specialist (CR94)
  • AP CSP Certiport Information Technology Specialist:
    • JavaScript (CR31)
  • Game Design Unity Certified User:
    • Programmer (CP58)
  • Systems Analysis & Design
    • CIW Advanced HTML5 & CSS3 Specialist (CR94)
    • CIW Site Development Associate (CB04)
  • Cybersecurity Certiport Information Technology Specialist:
    • Network Security (CR32)
    • CIW Web Security Associate (CB12)
    • CompTIA Security+ (CB44)
    • *if willing to do after school study, it is possible to gain the Google IT Support Professional Certificate (CP44)*
Summary

The Computer Science Career Technical Program is a better alternative to learn programming and a great way for learning how to work and collaborate with people and to be creative in a different way.
